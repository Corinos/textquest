# Asvith Silver-Step

## Character Concept
**Silent Rider**: A scout and cavalry warrior whose loyalty is unknown even to himself—playing both sides of the coming war.

## Bloodline & Affiliation
- **Bloodline**: Sarostiping (descendants of Sarostip Breaker-Of-Men, ~100 adults)
- **Occupation**: Thane, cavalry horse-lord, scout for the clan
- **Faction**: Wolfskinners (表面), but secretly Free Sartar (true loyalty)

## Background
Asvith was raised in the saddle. His family breeds the finest horses in the Red Cow—their horses are sought by warriors across Sartar. He can read the land from pony tracks, tell a rider's weight and mood from hoof-marks, and ride circles around any who chase.

The clans say the Sarostiping have horse-blood in their veins—and the clan doesn't disagree.

But Asvith carries a deeper secret. His father's death was not the hunting accident the clan was told. His father rode with Kallyr Starbrow's rebels, and was betrayed. The Lunar collaborator who sold him out? That man now sits on Broddi's council—the Moon Wind named Bolik Red-Turner.

Asvith knows. And he waits.

He rides for the Wolfskinners, serving Jomes Hostralos as a scout against the Telmori. This gives him access to the roads, the border, the movements of both Lunar forces and werewolves. His true loyalty is to the Free Sartar—to Kallyr Starbrow's rebellion. He feeds information to rebel contacts, and dreams of the day when the Lunars flee.

## Narrative Hooks
- **Father's Blood**: Knows who truly killed his father; waiting for the moment
- **Double Life**: Serves Jomes while feeding the rebellion
- **Horse-Speaker**: Can calm any mount; horses trust him absolutely

## Flaws

### Flaw 1: The Pretended Betrayer
**Rating**: 3 slots
His true loyalty must remain hidden. If discovered feeding the rebellion, he dies—and his family's honor dies with them. Every conversation is a performance.

### Flaw 2: Blood Debt
**Rating**: 2 slots
When he finally confronts Bolik Red-Turner, he must choose between duty to the rebellion and blood-right to kill him openly. The clan would condemn murder—but he has dreamed of it for years.
